Early Life and Career Beginnings

James Burton was born in Dubberly, Louisiana, and began playing guitar at a young age. His early influences included blues and country music, which would later shape his distinctive guitar playing style. Burton’s professional career began when he was just 14, playing guitar for local radio and TV shows in Shreveport, Louisiana.

Rise to Fame

Burton’s big break came when he was hired as a lead guitarist for Ricky Nelson’s band. His guitar solos on hits like “Hello Mary Lou” and “Travelin’ Man” helped establish him as one of the top guitarists in the industry. Burton’s work with Nelson significantly increased his visibility and led to collaborations with other major artists.

Working with Elvis Presley

In 1969, Burton received a call from Elvis Presley, who invited him to lead his new band, the TCB Band. Burton’s work with Presley further solidified his reputation as a top-notch guitarist. He continued to work with Presley until the singer’s death in 1977.

Post-Presley Career

After Presley’s death, Burton continued to work as a session musician and toured with various artists. He also released several solo albums and continued to influence a new generation of guitarists with his unique playing style.

Recognition and Awards

Over the years, Burton has received numerous awards and accolades for his contributions to music. In 2001, he was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. He has also been recognized by the Rockabilly Hall of Fame and the Musicians Hall of Fame.
