Maia Mitchell Biography

Maia Mitchell was born on August 18, 1992, in Lismore, New South Wales, and has worked diligently to become a television star from a young age. At age 14, she landed her first part as Brittany Flune on Mortified. Alex Mitchell, the taxi driver’s father of Maia Mitchell, and Jill Mitchell, the housewife’s mother, are the names of her parents. Charlie Mitchell is the name of her sister who is younger than she is.

She finished her early education at an Australian High School in Lismore. She then attended Trinity Catholic College in Lismore, where she received her diploma. She has been interested in singing and music since infancy and began playing guitar at a very early age. She has always desired a career in the film and television industries. Since then, Maia has landed prominent parts in films such as Teen Beach Movie and its sequel, as well as the vocal role in the Phineas and Ferb Movie.

Maia Mitchell Height, Weight

Mitchell has a height of 1.68 meters and a weight of around 54 kg, according to her physical measurements. She also has an hourglass body, with 33-24-34-inch measurements. In Australia, she wears a size 8 dress and a size 8.5 shoe. Additionally, she has dark brown eyes and dark brown hair.

Career

  • Mitchell started her acting career in 2006 with the lead part in Mortified, a step towards her professional goals. She appeared in many films at a very early age.
  • She portrayed Brittany Flune on Mortified for 25 episodes between 2006 and 2007. She resumed her acting career in 2008 with the lead part of Natasha Hamilton in Trapped.
  • In 2009, she again portrayed Taphony in the K-9 episode titled Taphony and the Time Loop.
  • In 2011, she reprised her role as Natasha Hamilton for 15 episodes of the television series Castaway. Her participation in several television programs has increased her popularity.
  • She portrayed Shaylee Michaels in the 2013 Jessie episode Jessie’s Big Break. In a similar manner, she debuted in the Disney Channel Original Movie Teen Beach Movie as McKenzie with Ross Lynch, Grace Phipps, Garrett Clayton, and Jordan Fisher. In addition, she portrayed Callie Adams Foster in the hit Freeform drama series The Fosters from 2013 to 2018, and her performance has been lauded.
  • During the fifth and last season of the program, it was revealed in June 2018 that she and Cierra Ramirez will be spun off into their own series named Good Trouble. Additionally, she worked with her partner Rudy Mancuso on the song “Magic.”
