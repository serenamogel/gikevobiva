Early Life and NBA Career

Reggie Miller was born on August 24, 1965, in Riverside, California. He played college basketball at UCLA before being drafted by the Indiana Pacers in 1987. Over his 18-year career with the Pacers, Miller became a five-time NBA All-Star and left an indelible mark on the league with his scoring ability, especially from beyond the arc. His NBA career earnings contributed significantly to his initial wealth accumulation.

Post-NBA Career and Broadcasting

After retiring from professional basketball in 2005, Miller transitioned into broadcasting. He has been a commentator for TNT’s NBA coverage, which has been a steady source of income for him. His articulate analysis and charismatic presence on screen have made him a favorite among viewers, further solidifying his financial stability post-retirement.
