Darrin Hartness Obituary And Death Cause

Posted by Martina Birk on Wednesday, July 24, 2024

The educational community is mourning the loss of Dr. Darrin L. Hartness, the President of Davidson-Davie Community College, who passed away on July 11, 2023, after a brave battle with cancer. His unwavering dedication to education in North Carolina and his compassionate leadership have left an indelible mark on countless lives. This article pays tribute to his remarkable career, his contributions to the field of education, and the legacy he leaves behind.

Who was Darrin Hartness?

Nội Dung Bài Viết:

Darrin Hartness, the former president of Davidson-Davie Community College, had an impressive career in public education spanning 27 years. In January 2019, he was appointed as the fourth president of Davidson-Davie Community College, marking a significant milestone in his professional journey.

Before assuming the role of college president, Hartness held various key positions in the field of education. Notably, he served as the superintendent of Mount Airy City Schools and Davie County Schools for a combined total of 11 years. Prior to that, he worked as an Assistant Professor, Chief Technology Officer, Assistant Superintendent, and Instructor, showcasing his wide-ranging expertise and commitment to advancing education.

Dr. Hartness earned his Ph.D. in Educational Leadership from the University of North Carolina at Charlotte, where he also obtained his Masters in Curriculum and Instructional Technology and Instructional Systems. His educational foundation was laid at Appalachian State University, where he completed his high school education and earned a bachelor’s degree in industrial technology.

Throughout his career, Hartness has been recognized for his outstanding contributions to education. In 2017, he was honored with the William and Ida Medal for his innovative approach to education. Additionally, he was selected to participate in the prestigious 2019 Aspen Presidential Fellowship, a program offered by the Aspen Institute in partnership with the Stanford Educational Leadership Initiative, exclusively for exceptional community college administrators.

In 2022, Hartness’s peers acknowledged his leadership abilities by electing him as the Secretary of the Executive Committee of the North Carolina Association of Community College Presidents, a role that further solidified his standing within the educational community.

Darrin Hartness’s remarkable career trajectory and numerous achievements underscore his unwavering dedication to education, making him a highly respected figure in the field.

Early Life and Education

Dr. Darrin Hartness, a native of North Carolina, exhibited a passion for education from a young age, inspired by the teachers who recognized his thirst for knowledge. He pursued higher education at esteemed institutions, earning a Bachelor’s degree in Education followed by a Master’s degree in Educational Leadership. He later completed his educational journey with a Doctorate in Education.

Transition to Professional Education

Upon completing his formal education, Dr. Hartness embarked on a career in education, driven by his desire to make a positive impact on students’ lives. He began as a teacher, imparting knowledge and instilling a love for learning in young minds. His dedication and innovative teaching methods garnered recognition, leading to promotions and increased responsibilities.

Superintendent and Administrator

Dr. Hartness’s exemplary leadership qualities became evident as he assumed roles such as principal, chief technology officer, and assistant superintendent. His ability to envision transformative changes in educational institutions resulted in successful tenures as the superintendent of Davie County Schools and Mount Airy City Schools. Through his leadership, he spearheaded initiatives that improved educational standards and fostered student success.

The Presidency at Davidson-Davie Community College

In January 2019, Dr. Darrin Hartness assumed the presidency of Davidson County Community College, which was later renamed Davidson-Davie Community College. His appointment marked the beginning of a new era for the college. With his visionary outlook, he aimed to create a community college that would empower students to achieve their academic and career goals.

Fostering a Culture of Compassion and Care

During his tenure at Davidson-Davie, Dr. Hartness prioritized fostering a culture of compassion and care among students, faculty, and staff. He recognized the importance of an inclusive and supportive environment for students to thrive academically and personally. Through various initiatives, he promoted mental health awareness, established mentoring programs, and encouraged community engagement.

Dr. Hartness’s leadership style was characterized by approachability and empathy. He believed in empowering his team and creating an atmosphere where everyone felt valued and heard. His genuine care for individuals made him a beloved figure within the college community.

On July 11, 2023, Darrin Hartness sadly passed away after a valiant battle with cancer. He had been diagnosed with esophageal cancer in December 2022 and had been fighting the disease with the support of his family and his faith. The entire educational community, as well as the people of North Carolina, mourn the loss of this esteemed educator.

The details of Darrin Hartness Obituary have not yet been released. As the news of his passing emerged only hours ago, the official obituary may take some time to be posted online. It is anticipated that his family will handle his last rites, and he will likely be cremated at a nearby crematorium.

Dr. Hartness’s family, friends, and community will find solace in the memories of his fulfilling life as they come to terms with their loss. They kindly request privacy during this time of mourning.

Darrin Hartness Obituary: Legacy and Impact

Dr. Darrin Hartness’s unwavering commitment to the North Carolina education system and his compassionate leadership have had a profound impact on the lives of countless individuals. His visionary approach to education, fostering a culture of compassion and care, has left a lasting legacy within Davidson-Davie Community College and the broader educational community.

In recognition of his 39 years of service and advocacy for public education and child-centered learning, community college presidents and public school superintendents across North Carolina have joined forces to establish the Hartness Memorial Scholarship Campaign. This scholarship fund aims to carry forward Dr. Hartness’s legacy by providing educational opportunities for deserving students.

Dr. Hartness leaves behind his beloved wife, Lisa, and their two accomplished children, Madison and Molly. Both Madison and Molly have followed in their parents’ footsteps, building successful careers in education.

As we remember Dr. Darrin L. Hartness, we honor his remarkable contributions to education and the lasting impact he has had on the lives of students and educators alike. His legacy will continue to inspire future generations in their pursuit of knowledge and personal growth.
