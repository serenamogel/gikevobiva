Andre Agassi Net Worth [2021 Update]

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Wednesday, June 5, 2024

The American former number one tennis player Andre Agassi sits at a net worth of $175 million.

Eight-time Grand Slam champion tennis player Andre Agassi hails from Nevada, Las Vegas, United States. In addition, he has won Olympic gold and all grand slams.

Likewise, Agassi was the first player to win four major Australian slams in the open era. Though, this record was broken by Novak Djokovic.

Moreover, he is amongst the five other male players in the open era to win the career grand slam.

Andre Agassi was born in 1970 to parents Emmanuel Agassi(father) and Elizabeth Agassi(mother).

Notably, at the age of 13, he went to Nick Bollettieri’s Tennis Academy just for three months because that is what his father could only afford.

Nonetheless, the coach has returned the paycheck to Andre’s father, saying he will stay here for free because the coach has seen the talent in Agassi.

But, he was dropped from School in 9th grade as he has to pursue a career in tennis.

Exceptionally, he is among only two male players to win the “Career golden slam” that features career slams and Olympic gold medals, and other to do so is Rafael Nadal.

Surprisingly, Agassi is the only player to win a ‘career super slam” that includes all four majors, Olympic gold, and one ATP title.

Andre Agassi Career

As mentioned, Andre started playing tennis from an early age. Furthermore, Agassi started playing as a junior player.

Agassi won the first championship at 12. Moving forward, he joined Nick Bolleriterri’s Academy.

This led his coach to be highly impressed with Agassi’s ability. Then he turned into a professional player at the age of 16 and won $2 million in prize money by the next two years.

He held the record of the most consecutive win by a teenager back then. Later, after 17 years, the record was broken by Rafael Nadal.

Interestingly, he refused to take part in Wimbledon from 1988 to 1990 as he was not happy with the rule that players need to wear a white dress.

Andre has formed many rivalries with Sampras, Becker, Chang, Federer, and Lendl. Furthermore, he claimed world number 1 rank in 1995 and even went on to win the Olympic gold in 1996.

Agassi did not appear in the Australian Open for eight consecutive years in his early professional career.

Meanwhile, he has won four Australian grand slam titles. Similarly, he went on to win Wimbledon, French Open, and US Open.
